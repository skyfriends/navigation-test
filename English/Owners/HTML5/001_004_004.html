4.4 Extruder System Normal Startup Sequence

To initiate a PLC-managed extruder system normal startup, the operator brings up the Overview screen on the control room APM console, clicks the “(F5) Extruder Commands” button, and on the Commands Menu dialog box that appears, clicks the Start Extruder button. The sequence of operations that the PLC executes when the user chooses a normal startup of the extruder system are listed below in the order that they take place.

that this control scheme uses the gravimetric (loss in weight) mode of feeder control, which is the default and preferred mode, rather than the volumetric mode.

  1. The PLC causes the alarm horn to sound and the beacon to flash for about ten seconds to alert all personnel that the extruder system is about to start. At the same time the PLC displays the system status in the upper left corner of the APM monitor as “Starting Extruder.”
  2. The PLC positions the downspout between the preconditioner outlet and the extruder inlet in the bypass position.
  3. The PLC starts the extruder's lubrication oil pump.
  4. The PLC fills the live bin with dry feed from the surge bin via the surge bin slide gate.
  5. The PLC starts the live bin agitator.
  6. The PLC waits until the weight of dry feed in the live bin reaches a target weight value that was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup. While the live bin fills the system status that is displayed in the upper left corner of the APM monitor will be displayed as “Waiting for Live Bin to Fill.” NOTE: that the user can terminate waiting for the live bin to fill (although not recommended) by clicking the (F6) Bypass/Confirm Menu button on the Overview screen, and on the Bypass/Confirm Commands Menu dialog box that appears, clicking the Bypass Live Bin Fill button. NOTE: also that during normal operation the PLC automatically refills the live bin whenever the weight of dry feed material in the live bin falls below a value that was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup.
  7. The PLC enables automatic control of the extruder barrel zone temperatures to prewarm the extruder barrel while the live bin is filling. Steam or cooling water is applied by the PLC to each of the extruder barrel temperature control zones to attain the respective setpoint temperatures. Throughout normal operation the PLC continuously monitors and maintains the respective extruder barrel zone temperatures. The respective extruder barrel zone temperatures are displayed, and the respective setpoint changed, on the Barrel screen on the APM monitor.
  8. The PLC starts the preconditioner in the forward direction.
  9. When the PLC verifies that the preconditioner is running it starts the screw feeder. Initially for a short time, the PLC calculates the feeder speed from the current formula's dry feed bulk density, startup feed rate, and feed rate setpoint. But in short order, the PLC uses live bin loss in weight to determine the feeder speed.
  10. As soon as the PLC confirms that the screw feeder is running it displays the Confirm Knife ready button on the Overview screen a button above, below or adjacent to the knife. At the same time the system status that is displayed in the upper left corner of the APM monitor will be displayed as “Waiting for Knife Confirmation.” When the user is certain that it is safe for the knife to start, he clicks the Confirm Knife Ready button, at which point the PLC starts the knife.
  11. After the PLC verifies that the screw feeder is running, and after a time delay (which may be zero) that was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup, the PLC enables both steam and water flow to the preconditioner, the magnitude of each being a respective percentage of the dry feed rate, as per the current formula values loaded into the PLC. Water is injected into the preconditioner through a bank of spray nozzles. These spray nozzles ensure a uniform distribution of water at all flow rates. The PLC selects the water nozzle(s) appropriate given the current water flow rate setpoint. These nozzles are automatically switched on and off by the PLC as needed throughout the extruder system's operation.
  12. After the PLC verifies that the screw feeder is running, and after a time delay (which may be zero) that was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup, the PLC enables all liquids to flow to the preconditioner, the magnitude of each being a respective percentage of the dry feed rate, as per the current formula values loaded into the PLC. As with water, product-specific liquids may be injected into the preconditioner through spray nozzles. NOTE: that the number of liquids that are used is customer and product dependent. Some customers have products with formulations that require multiple liquids, while other customers have products that require no liquids (other than water).
  13. When the actual water flow rate to the preconditioner becomes greater than the preconditioner water flow rate setpoint minus its alarm offset value, the PLC begins a time delay to allow the preconditioner to fill with sufficiently mixed material. During this time delay the system status that is displayed in the upper left corner of the APM monitor will be displayed as “Waiting for Cylinder to Fill.” At the same time, a “Bypass Preconditioner Fill Time” button will appear and remain on the Overview screen until the preconditioner fill time delay counts down to zero. The operator can terminate the preconditioner fill time delay and cause the normal startup sequence to proceed to the next step by clicking this button. This is useful if the normal startup began with premixed material already in the preconditioner.
  14. The PLC enables process water to flow to the extruder, the magnitude being a percentage of the dry feed rate, as per the current formula value loaded into the PLC. NOTE: that this water is injected into the extruder; it is not water that is used for extruder barrel temperature control. During startup, an extra amount of water is added, as per the current formula value loaded into the PLC. Once the PLC considers the startup sequence to be complete it no longer adds this extra amount of water to the extruder.
  15. When the actual water flow rate to the extruder becomes greater than the extruder water flow rate setpoint minus its alarm offset value, the PLC starts the extruder motor at a speed that is 30 percent of the formula final extruder speed setpoint. The PLC will only start the extruder motor if it confirms the flow of lubrication oil. The PLC will terminate the normal startup sequence if lubrication oil flow is not confirmed within a time interval that was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup.
  16. The PLC enables process steam to flow to the extruder, the magnitude being a percentage of the dry feed rate, as per the current formula value loaded into the PLC. NOTE: that this steam is injected into the extruder; it is not steam that is used for extruder barrel temperature control.
  17. The PLC waits for a time delay to expire that was started when the extruder motor was started. When the time delay expires, the PLC sets the downspout position to channel mixed feed material from the preconditioner outlet into the extruder inlet.
  18. The PLC attempts to confirm that the knifehood is in place and connected. If this is not so an alarm will appear and the PLC will not proceed with the startup sequence until it has confirmed that the knifehood has been fastened together.
  19. The PLC starts the knife, assuming that the operator has confirmed that the knife is safe to start. The operator is first prompted to confirm that the knife is safe to start earlier in this sequence when the screw feeder starts running. If the operator has not confirmed that the knife is safe to start, the PLC will wait until it receives confirmation.
  20. The PLC starts the knife, and sets its speed to the formula knife speed setpoint. The operator may increase or decrease the knife speed by changing the knife speed setpoint on the Formula screen or by using the knife speed selector switch mounted on the knife stand near the extruder. The knife speed will slowly ramp up or down if the selector switch is held in the increase or decrease position, respectively. The knife speed will change in small increments if the operator quickly rotates and releases the selector switch.
  21. The PLC ramps the dry feed rate setpoint from the formula startup feed rate setpoint to the formula normal operation dry feed rate setpoint.
  22. The PLC waits for a time delay to expire that was started when the downspout was positioned to channel mixed feed material to the extruder inlet. When the time delay expires the PLC no longer adds extra startup process water to the extruder.
  23. The PLC's normal startup sequence is concluded when the PLC confirms that all extruder system devices are running, and that the flow rates for all product ingredients (dry feed, water, steam, liquids) reach have attained their respective formula setpoint values.

On some twin screw extruder models, there is an auxiliary oil filter pump, and the PLC starts it one hour after it starts the extruder motor.
