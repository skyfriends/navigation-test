4.6 Extruder System Normal Shutdown Sequence

To initiate an PLC-managed extruder system normal shutdown, the operator brings up the Overview screen on the control room APM console, clicks the “(F5) Extruder Commands” button, and on the Commands Menu dialog box that appears, clicks the “Normal Shutdown” button. The normal shutdown sequence of operations that the PLC executes are listed below in the order that they take place.

  1. The PLC displays the system status in the upper left corner of the APM monitor “Stopping Extruder.”
  2. The PLC ceases refilling the surge bin and live bin if they are at or below low level.
  3. The PLC increases the flow rate of process water to the extruder by the same formula value of extra extruder water for startup.
  4. The PLC stops the screw feeder. The PLC also causes a button with the caption “Bypass Preconditioner Empty Time” to be displayed on the Overview screen.
  5. The PLC stops the live bin agitator.
  6. The PLC waits for a time delay to expire that was started when the screw feeder stopped. The length of this time delay was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup. When this time delay expires, the PLC stops the flow of process water, process steam and all liquids to the preconditioner.
  7. The PLC waits for another time delay to expire that was started when the screw feeder stopped. The length of this time delay was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup. When this time delay expires, the PLC positions the downspout to the bypass position for about two seconds. The operator can skip this delay at any time by clicking the “Bypass Preconditioner Empty Time” button.
  8. The PLC stops the flow of process steam to the extruder.
  9. The PLC positions the downspout to the process position for about five seconds.
  10. The PLC positions the downspout to the bypass position for the remainder of the shutdown sequence.
  11. The PLC places the knifehood diverter in the Reject position. The PLC ignores the signal from the knifehood diverter switch located near the extruder for the remainder of the shutdown sequence.
  12. The PLC stops the preconditioner.
  13. The PLC waits for a time delay to expire that was started when the downspout was placed in the Bypass position for the last time. The length of this time delay was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup. When this time delay expires, the PLC stops the flow of process water to the extruder.
  14. The PLC waits for another time delay to expire that was started when the downspout was placed in the Bypass position for the last time. The length of this time delay was configured by the Wenger Technicians during initial installation and system startup. When this time delay expires, the PLC stops the extruder motor.
  15. The PLC stops the knife.
  16. The PLC stops the extruder lubrication pump.
  17. The PLC stops the extruder lubrication auxiliary oil filter pump.
  18. The PLC stops the flow of process water to the extruder.
  19. The PLC stops the flow of barrel temperature control water and steam to the extruder.
  20. The PLC's normal startup sequence is concluded.

During the shutdown sequence the operator can restart the extruder by clicking the Start Extruder button on the Command Menu dialog box, up to the point when the downspout is placed in the Bypass position for the first time. Thereafter, the operator must wait for the shutdown sequence to conclude before starting the extruder again. If the operator chooses to restart the extruder during a shutdown, all required devices will be set in the run mode immediately. There will not be any sequence associated with this restart mode.

After the extruder system is shutdown, the operator should set the knife disconnect switch to the Off position before removing the knife safety shield and before removing the knife drive shaft from the knife bearing assembly.
