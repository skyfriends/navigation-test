1 General Information

1.1 Document Overview

This APM Control System User manual document is intended to be useful in training new users how to operate their Wenger extruder systems. It is also intended as a reference for experienced users.

Each major APM software screen is presented and discussed in a separate chapter, with each chapter focusing on what a new, as opposed to an experienced, operator will need to know about how to use that APM software screen. Next comes a chapter that explains the details of some of the major PLC-supervised automated sequences that most users will rely upon to operate their extruder systems. Addressed also in this chapter are some PLC-supervised automated utility sequences that may be used on occasion. Lastly there is a chapter addressing the subject of APM alarms - the four classes of alarms, and a list of the specific alarms that will be common to most if not all Wenger extruder systems.

This document is not meant to be a replacement for a qualified Wenger controls specialist on site to startup your equipment. All Wenger APM controlled equipment is required to be started up by properly trained Wenger Controls Technician.

When a dialog box is referenced this indicates you are working in a Windows-developed software, and when a faceplate is referenced this indicates you are working in a Wenger-developed software.
