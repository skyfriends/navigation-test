4.12 Other Operations

4.12.1 Emergency-Stopping

Each extruder system features two emergency stop (E-Stop) pushbuttons. One is on the control room console and the other is on a small control panel located near the extruder downspout. These pushbuttons have large red actuator heads. E-Stop is activated by pushing the red actuator head down (it should lock in place). E-Stop is deactivated by twisting the red actuator head until it pops back up.

Either E-Stop pushbutton will terminate output power from the PLC to all extruder system electrical equipment. Outputs signals from the PLC to all motors, valves, and flow control devices will be interrupted. These devices cannot be energized while the E-Stop pushbutton remains in the down position.

4.12.2 Changing the Downspout Position

Each extruder system features two manual downspout bypass switches. One is on the control room console and the other is on a small control panel located near the extruder downspout. Either of these two switches will allow the operator to reposition the downspout from channeling preconditioner effluent into the extruder inlet. These switches are ignored when the downspout is already in the bypass position.

4.12.3 Changing the Knife Speed

A switch is mounted on the knife stand for controlling the knife speed. This switch can be used to both increase and decrease the knife speed. The switch can be rotated clockwise or counterclockwise from its middle (off) position, but when released will always return to its off position. Rotating and holding the switch counterclockwise will cause the knife speed to slowly ramp up, while rotating and holding the switch clockwise will cause the knife speed to slowly ramp down.

4.12.4 Recovering from Excessive Extruder Motor Load

If the extruder motor load or the conehead pressure exceeds their respective high alarm limit value for a configured period of time, the PLC will initiate the overload recovery routine in an attempt to lower the load. The extruder water setpoint will be increased by 1%, the downspout will divert to reject then back to the extruder 3 times. If the load remains high through all these corrective actions, the PLC will initiate a quick shutdown. If these measures are effective in decreasing the load the extruder will continue to run. The 1% additional water is removed upon acknowledgement and clearing of the alarm. When the alarm condition is corrected and the operator has acknowledged the alarm the 1% water will automatically be removed.
