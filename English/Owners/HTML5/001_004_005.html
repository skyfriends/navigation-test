4  Programmable Logic Controller

4.5 Extruder System Normal Operation After Startup

After PLC executes the extruder system's startup sequence to completion the extruder is in its normal steady-state operation mode. From this point on, until an extruder shutdown sequence is initiated, the PLC attempts to maintain the actual measured values of the following entities at their setpoint values:

  • Dry feed rate to the preconditioner.
  • Process water flow rate to the preconditioner.
  • Process steam flow rate to the preconditioner.
  • Liquid(s) flow rate to the preconditioner.
  • Process water flow rate to the extruder.
  • Process steam flow rate to the extruder.
  • Extruder zone temperatures.

For the entities above requiring flow rate control, the operator specifies the desired flow rate of all process water, process steam and liquids as respective percentages of the dry feed rate. From these percentages, the PLC calculates the desired mass flow rate of each ingredient, it continuously measures the actual mass flow rate for each ingredient, and it uses an implementation of a Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) control algorithm to maintain the actual mass flow for each ingredient at its respective desired mass flow rate.

The PLC also uses PID schemes for the control of the various extruder barrel temperatures. The PLC monitors the extruder's various barrel zone thermocouples, one thermocouple for each extruder zone (the temperature of the mixed material in the downspout is also measured, but only to be displayed, not for temperature control). The PLC controls each zone extruder barrel temperature with steam and water to the jacket of that zone, steam for heating and water for cooling.
